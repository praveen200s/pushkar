Hues of Pushkar Fair

A Reflection on Colors than Camels

( NOTE : Best viewed on a tablet or a desktop. )

The Pushkar Fair, also called the Pushkar Camel Fair or locally as Kartik Mela or Pushkar ka Mela is an annual multi-day livestock fair and cultural fÃªte held in the town of Pushkar (Rajasthan, India).

In 1998, over 1 million visitors came to Pushkar throughout the year.The Pushkar fair alone attracts over 200,000 visitors.

We visited the Pushkar fair in 2019, with a purpose of unearthing the depth & breadth of colors, seen all around the festive ground. We documented the market, the lanes, the vendors & ofcourse the camel fair.

We wanted to undestand the local trend in colors.How different or similar is it to global trends ? Do they influence eachother or does the local choices stay intact over the years.

We put 2019 Pushkar Fair as our first anchor for the study. We curated almost more than 2000 photographs and selected 500 images for the analysis.

We used a mix of tools, custom & few already available to us and extracted color pallete from each of the images.We narrowed down 5 colors for the pallete.Like the first color shown here.

We created a datasheet for each image along with its 5 colors palette.

At the end, we had 2500 colors.

To move ahead, we simply aligned them along 2 axes and observed the bands/groups/clusters of colors. There was not scale to axis as colors are inherently intuitive data.

First, we mapped HUE values vs Saturation levels.

Clearly there was a marked clusters of the Red-Violet hues

Second, we plotted Saturation levels vs Brightness.

Colors lined up with intervals of brigtness values with each separated by ~10-11 points.

Third, we plotted Hue values vs Brightness.

Although there was clear intervals of brightness points, the low saturated colors were dispersed over a continuous section in comparison to high saturated colors.

The HSB plots did gave little hints to us about the color clusters. But we wanted to see the richness in our pallete also.

So we placed all the colors togther & made clusters as per each color index of the pallete.The potpurri of colors was a delight.

Further to ascertain the frequancy of colors in our palletes, we seggragated the colors on a hue scale with 8 specific hue points.

The sparse & thick colors showed up naturally in a lovely form.
